About me

I am a PhD student at the University of Padova, Department of Information Engineering, where I began my doctoral journey on October 1st, 2023. My research is focused on the title "Machine Learning approaches for sustainable mobility," exploring how intelligent systems can contribute to more sustainable and efficient transportation solutions. I am also an active member of the AMCO (Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Control) research lab, where I develop a strong interest in Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning, both in theory and practical applications.

I earned my Master’s Degree in Control Systems Engineering from the University of Padova in 2023, and my Bachelor’s Degree in Information Engineering from the same institution in 2021.

Additionally, I have been involved in the Bosch Future Mobility Challenge for two consecutive years — as a team member in 2023 and as a mentor in 2024. In both editions, our team achieved second place, which reflects my commitment to collaborative innovation and sustainable mobility solutions.
